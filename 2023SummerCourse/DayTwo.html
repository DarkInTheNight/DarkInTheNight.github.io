Air Pressure

Today's lesson was a busy one! I taught the class about gas particles and the air pressure they exert, giving them examples such as the Magdeburg hemispheres. Then, I gave them 3 demonstrations: water staying in an upside down cup, an egg falling into and out of a small-mouthed bottle, and a can being crushed by air. The lesson ended with the children building and racing each other with balloon-powered cars. Another exciting day!

As always, the lesson began with an explanation on the topic: air pressure.

One simple yet shocking demonstration of air pressure's power is its ability to keep water in an upside-down cup.

Air pressure can also be used to get an egg in and out of a bottle!

The final demonstration used air pressure to crush a can.

The final task was a fun one as the kids built and raced air-powered ballon cars!
